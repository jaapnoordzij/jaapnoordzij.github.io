The Revenant

Iñárritu's stories can be quite impressive. I liked Babel, 21 Grams and Biutiful very much and although I still haven't seen Birdman I believe his films tend to become more and more poetic and surrealistic. At least that is what I was expecting when I went to see The Revenant. But after a week's reflection I still don't know whether Iñárritu follows this path further or not. Sure, there are dreamlike sequences in the film that show a poetic, almost "Tarkovsky-like" atmosphere but they are placed in an context of extreme violence and hatred. For me this makes the film a bit unbalanced or should I say 'unsettling'.

The story of Hugh Glass in itself is impressive enough so it is not clear why Iñárritu had to polish it into a more sentimental setting where Glass has gone 'native' and has a son. It also looks like he made John Fitzpatrick (Tom Hardy) more or less pure evil. His character evokes no sympathy whatsoever in the story and his only role seems to be to cause unlimited fear and hatred in other people.

The landscapes are spectecular and of course the fight with the grizzly in the beginning of the film is very impressive to watch but for the rest I found the film somewhat disappointing.
