A Burnt-Out Case - Graham Greene

Graham Greene wrote this novel after several travels into Africa. Like in most of his books, also in this book the Christian faith plays an important role. Greene's characters usualy totter on the brink between belief and non-belief. They are educated as proper Catholics but in the course of their lives they loose their faith to some extend.

The "world famous architect" Querry is a good example of this kind. He used to believe in God and go to church but as he grows older he discovers that his "love" is mainly a love for himself and has very little to do with a transcending love. This is not only true for his relation to God but also for his relation to women. He has always enjoyed women and has had many relationships. However after many years he discovers that he has not love at all. That brings him to a deep crisis, a kind of "burn-out" from life.

Querry ends up on a remote leprosy colony deep in the Congolese jungle. There he find some rest and acceptance. Neither doctor Colin (an atheist) nor the local Catholic fathers as too many questions and in helping out in the clinic Querry discovers that his life maybe not lost after all.
